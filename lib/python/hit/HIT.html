The goal of this study

Siri, Alexa and other language technologies often have trouble with the speech patterns of people from many underrepresented groups, including the Black community. In this project, we would like to help these technologies better recognize and appropriately respond to what is known as African American Vernacular English (AAVE). If you are a native AAVE speaker, this is how you can help us:

  1. You can decide whether bits of automatically-generated (fake) AAVE text are understandable and whether it is possible that someone could say it, at least in theory.
  2. You can help us "translate" sentences from SAE to AAVE.

What exactly is AAVE?

AAVE is the variety of English that is spoken, especially in more casual settings, by many working- and middle-class African Americans.

SAE and AAVE pairs.

  1. SAE: She is always telling people she’s eight.
    AAVE: She be telling people she eight.
  2. SAE: They aren’t going bad. They have been bad for a long time
    AAVE: They ain’t going bad. They been bad.
  3. SAE: They will have already grown out of that by then.
    AAVE: They a be done growed out that by then.
  4. SAE: Are you getting ready/about to eat?
    AAVE: Y'all finna eat?
  5. SAE: Those students are always working diligently to make money
    AAVE: Them students be steady trying to make a buck.
